Track My Life

Yilin Ying's Exporation in Mobile Data

Byte 3 for Interactive Data Science

1. Introduction

1.1. Project Background

In this project, I have collected mobile data on my phone by using AWARE. I used google cloud platform SQL to store my data, Trifacta Wrangler to clean my data, finally google fusion table and plotly to visualize my data. I hope to learn more about myself, be more aware of my life pattern and live mindfully through this visualization project.

1.2. Data Description

My data range from 2/2/2017 to 2/23/2017. Overall, I have location data presesnted in latitude and longitude, screen status data (whether the screen is on or locked), environment noise data presesnted in sound energy and battery data. Each datapoint has a seperate timestamp. With these data, I can easily track my daily activity, environment noise and phone usage by time.

2. Where Do I Go?

In this part I visualized my location data by using google fusion table.

2.1. My Oscillation of Life

The two maps below separately show how my latitude and longitude change over time. According to the map, there is a daily wave in weekdays. That is because I go to CMU every day for school. CMU is famous for its high workload. Thus, my life is quite a "home, school" routine --- but still enjoyable. In weekends, I love to walk around my house or sleep at home, but seldom go to school. Since I don't have a car, it is not very easy to travel far away. Because of that, my activity diameter for weekends is really small.

2.2. Track Me in a Map

Below are showing my traces in maps. According to the heat map, the two places that I appear most frequently are CMU and my house in Greenfield. I take 61C or 61D for school every day. Because of that, there is a trace on Murray and Forves Avenue overlapping the bus routes. Also, I have been to Aldi in Waterfont, a restaurant at North Craig Street, Giant Eagle near my house, Carnegie Library, etc. in the past few days. These places are all shown in the maps.

In the second map, yellow dots represent my location from 0 am to 9 am. Green dots are from 9 am to 7 pm. Purple dots are from 7 pm to 0 am. We can clearly see that from 9 am to 7 pm I am often at school. In other time, I am more likely to be at home.

3. Noise in My Life

I also collected the sound energy data. These data can show the environment noise around me, so as to indicate when and where is often noisy or quiet for me. There might be several reasons if the sound value is large: I am talking to other people; I am using my phone to play music; the environment is noisy. Sadly, we cannot distinct different types of noise sources from each other. This is one limitation of this visualization.

3.1. When is Noisy?

I put all the sound energy data by hours and dates in a scattered plot. The size of each point represents the volume of sound energy. The larger is the point, the more noisy is the environment.

According to this visualization, it is quiet in most of my bedtime -- often around 0-8 a.m. The only outlier is the day of 2/22/2017, when the whole night is noisy. My guess is that probably I played music on my phone for the whole night.

Another discovery is that weekends (2/4, 2/5, 2/11, 2/18, 2/19) are often quieter than weekdays. The reason behind is possibly that I don't need to interact with other people in weekends. As an introvert person, I feel more energetic when I am alone, but my major requires me to talk to people in group discussions or interviews. Because of that, it is really helpful to have a quiet weekend each week to get my energy back.

3.2. Where is Noisy?

In this graph, I put the Noise Plot under latitude and longitude. With this graph we can see where is generally noisy or quiet in my life. The two clusters in the plot is CMU (upper left) and my home (lower right). There are noisy dots in both CMU and my home. It is probably because I need to talk to my classmates in school and talk to my roommates at home. I also have quiet study time at home or on campus.

4. How do I Play with My Phone?

In this part, I visualize my phone screen status and battery data to present my phone usage.

4.1. My Phone Usage

The graph below shows the number of action to unlock a screen for each hour over the day. AWARE can collect 4 different types screen status: screen on, screen off, lock and unlock. I choose to visualize hourly unlock number here because "unlock" shows my active usage of mobile phone. In comparison, "screen on" also happens when I shake my phone or when I receive a message, and thus may involve some noise in the result.

According to the result, most of the usage happens from 9 am to 9 pm. This is general work and school time when I use my mobile phone to contact my classmates, browse news, look for information and send emails. In most days, I am not using my phone in 0-8am, because that is my general bedtime. Every day before going to bed, I need to set an alarm; after I wake up, I need to turn off the alarm. Thus, the first/last usage of a day can present when I get up and when I get to sleep. An extreme example is in 2/22 when my first usage of phone happened in 2 am. That is because I slept earlier that night and then woke up at 2 and felt energetic again.

An advantage to use unlock data is to visualize how my life is interupted by mobile phone. For example, I unlocked my phone for 9 times in 10-11 am, 2/18, probably because of message, or just because I was not focusing on my work and my mind kept flying around to the mobile phone. I am so guilty that I use my phone so many times every day and so addicted to the virtural world. I should have been more focused on other aspects of life.

4.2. Battery

The heat map shows the battery status of my phone. I usually get my phone charged once a day. An interesting fact is that the battery goes down fast if it is not charged even from 0-8 am when I am usually sleeping. The possible reason behind is that I am using AWARE to collect data. The sensors are still running even when I am sleeping.
